The Twenty-Seventh Day of the Fifth Moon, Year of the Dragon
The Oracle's Response
A Reading from the I Ching, the Book of Changes
HEXAGRAM XXIII — SPLITTING APART
○
×
Changing Lines: Line 1 (Old Yang) • Line 5 (Old Yin)

To the Seeker who has cast these coins with a sincere heart,

The Oracle has spoken through the ancient pattern of the Splitting Apart (剝), the twenty-third hexagram of the Book of Changes. The mountain rests upon the earth, and the gentle wind of decay touches all things. This is not a sign of destruction, but of the natural cycle wherein the old must yield to make way for the new.

The bed is split apart at its legs. Those who are firm and correct will meet with disaster. Yet if they remain still and do not act, they shall find fortune in the end.

The changing lines reveal the path of transformation. The Old Yang at the foundation speaks of a strength that has reached its limit — like a mountain eroded by wind and rain, what once stood firm now crumbles. The Old Yin at the summit whispers of a gentleness that has endured too long, inviting the necessary dissolution.

In matters of your inquiry, the Oracle advises patience above all. The mountain does not resist the wind; it yields gradually, and in that yielding, preserves its essence. What appears as loss is but the shedding of that which no longer serves. Do not grasp at what is departing, for your hands must be empty to receive what approaches.

The superior person takes heed of the signs of decay and fortifies the spirit. By understanding the pattern of decline, one may prepare for the return.

The second hexagram, revealed by the transformation of the changing lines, speaks of Return (復). After the mountain has yielded, the thunder awakens beneath the earth. The light returns after darkness, as it has done since the beginning of time. Your path, though it passes through shadow, leads inevitably toward renewal.

Cast your coins again when the moon has completed her cycle, should you seek further counsel. The Oracle does not speak twice to the same question, for wisdom is wasted upon the impatient.

The Oracle
KEEPER OF THE BOOK OF CHANGES
